The Story

He quit his job, converted an old Opel, and drove toward the unknown

Bobby Petrov wasn't supposed to be here — parked on a red dirt road somewhere between the Sahara and the Atlantic, sleeping in the back of a car older than most of the people reading this. He was supposed to be on a Zoom call. Answering Slack messages. Living the safe, predictable life he'd built working remotely for a company in England.

But something kept pulling. A restlessness that no salary could quiet. So he did what most people only dream about: he quit. He ripped up the script. He took his battered 1995 Opel Astra — a car he'd already converted into the world's most unlikely micro-camper — and pointed it south.

First came the coasts of Croatia and Italy. Then the wild edges of Spain and Portugal. And then, on a ferry from Europe to Tangier, he crossed into Africa — and into a completely different chapter of his life. Morocco became home for months. The Astra parked in deserts, next to oceans, beside mountain roads. And when Morocco felt familiar, he kept going. Mauritania. Senegal. The Gambia. And now, Sierra Leone.

This book is the unfiltered story of that journey. The breakdowns and the breakthroughs. The loneliness and the strangers who became friends. The fear and the freedom. Written from the driver's seat of a car that was never meant to make it this far — just like the man behind the wheel.
